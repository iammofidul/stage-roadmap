üìâ Current Problem:

Can we track users from special access to payment? NO
Do we know which feature sequence converts best? NO
Can we measure: "Users who unlock special access have X% higher retention"? NO

üéØ Why This Initiative?

The Problem: Features (special access, spin wheel) track events independently. We can't answer critical questions like:

  • "Do users who unlock special access convert to paid at higher rates?"
  • "Does Special Access ‚Üí Payment convert better than Spin Wheel ‚Üí Payment?"
  • "What's the typical trial user journey from activation to payment?"

The Solution: Add a unique journey ID to every trial user that links all their events. This creates a "breadcrumb trail" from trial start to payment (or churn).

‚úÖ What We'll Do:

  • Generate unique journey ID when user activates trial
  • Add journey ID to all feature events: special access claims, spin wheel interactions, payment attempts
  • Create milestone events: special access claim, spin wheel claim, first payment attempt
  • Store journey ID locally so it persists across app sessions

üìà How This Improves Funnels:

Direct Impact: No immediate funnel improvement (this is infrastructure)

Enables Future Wins:

  • Identify high-converting feature sequences ‚Üí prioritize showing those
  • Find drop-off points in journey ‚Üí fix them
  • Measure: "Users who claim special access on Day 1 have 40% higher D7 retention" ‚Üí optimize timing
  • All Month 2-3 optimizations depend on this tracking

Why First? Without journey tracking, we're flying blind. This unlocks data-driven decisions for everything else.

#2
Video Completion Rate Optimization
Week 2-4 2 Developers +7pp Video Completion

üéØ Target Users:

trial_active subscription_active

üìä Amplitude Data Reference:

Chart: 90% Video Completion Chart
Chart ID: q2dd1ib2

üìâ Current Amplitude Data:

Users who start videos 204,259
Users who reach 90% completion 113,229 (55%)
Drop-off before completion 91,030 users (45%)
Median time to start playback 24 seconds (TOO SLOW)

üéØ Why This Initiative?

The Problem: 45% of users abandon videos before 90% completion. This is a retention killer because:

  • Users who complete videos have 2-3x higher Day-7 retention
  • Incomplete videos = unsatisfied users = churn
  • You're losing engagement from half your active users

Root Causes:

  • Slow start: 24 second median wait time ‚Üí users give up
  • Lost context: User starts video, exits app, forgets what they were watching
  • Friction: No easy way to continue where they left off
  • No momentum: Video ends ‚Üí user has to manually find next content ‚Üí drops off

The Solution: Reduce friction at every step of the viewing experience.

‚úÖ What We'll Do:

  • Resume Watching Banner on Home: If user has partially watched videos (10-80% complete), show prominent banner with thumbnail + progress bar ‚Üí One-tap to continue
  • Auto-Play Next Content: When video ends, show 5-second countdown to next episode/recommendation ‚Üí Removes manual search friction
  • Skip Intro Button: For series content, detect intro section (first 60-90 seconds) ‚Üí Show "Skip Intro" button ‚Üí Get to content faster
  • Optimize Buffering: Reduce initial buffering from 24s ‚Üí <3s ‚Üí Track and measure startup time improvements
  • Progress Bars on Thumbnails: Show visual progress on all content cards (e.g., "75% watched" badge) ‚Üí User remembers what they were watching

üìà How This Improves Funnels:

Video Completion Funnel Impact:

Video Start ‚Üí 90%: 55% ‚Üí Video Start ‚Üí 90%: 62%
Result: +7pp improvement = 14,314 more users completing videos (per month)

Downstream Funnel Effects:

  • D0 Consumption: Resume banner helps lapsed D0 users return ‚Üí 66% ‚Üí 68% (+2pp)
  • Trial D1 Retention: Users who complete 2+ videos have 35% higher D1 retention ‚Üí 27% ‚Üí 30% (+3pp)
  • Engagement Depth: Auto-play next increases videos per session ‚Üí Higher engagement score ‚Üí Better retention

Why Prioritize? Video completion is a leading indicator of retention. Fix this early ‚Üí compound effects throughout trial period.

üìâ Current Measurement Gap:

Special access offers shown UNKNOWN
Claim rate (offer ‚Üí claim) UNKNOWN
Watch rate (claim ‚Üí video start) UNKNOWN
Completion rate (start ‚Üí 90%) UNKNOWN
Payment conversion (SA ‚Üí paid) UNKNOWN

üéØ Why This Initiative?

The Problem: Special Access is a premium engagement feature but you have NO VISIBILITY into its effectiveness:

  • You know users see offers, but not HOW MANY claim them
  • Don't know if claimed special access leads to higher retention
  • Can't measure: "Special access on Day 1 vs Day 3 - which converts better?"
  • Missing: Drop-off analysis (where do users abandon?)

Business Critical: If special access is a retention driver, you need to:

  • Show it to more users (if it works)
  • Show it at optimal time (Day 0? Day 1? Day 3?)
  • Fix drop-offs (if claim rate is low, optimize offer)
  • Prove ROI: "Special access users convert at X% higher rate"

The Solution: Track complete funnel from offer shown to payment conversion.

‚úÖ What We'll Do:

  • Complete Funnel Events: Track 5-step funnel: Offer shown ‚Üí Offer claimed ‚Üí Video started ‚Üí Video 90% ‚Üí Video completed
  • Content Context: Add content ID, title, genre to all events ‚Üí Know which content works best
  • Timing Analysis: Track which trial day special access was claimed (Day 0, 1, 2...7) ‚Üí Find optimal timing
  • Claim Speed: Track seconds between offer shown and claim button pressed ‚Üí Measure urgency
  • Payment Correlation: Track if user paid within 7 days of claiming special access ‚Üí Prove conversion lift
  • Trigger Source: Was special access shown by manual trigger or back-press experiment? ‚Üí Measure which source works best

üìà How This Improves Funnels:

No Immediate Funnel Impact (Measurement Setup)

Enables These Critical Decisions:

Decision 1: Should we show special access to more users?

  • If claim rate >40% AND claimers have +30% higher D7 retention ‚Üí Yes, expand
  • If claim rate <20% OR no retention lift ‚Üí No, optimize or deprioritize

Decision 2: When should we show special access?

  • Compare: Day 0 claims vs Day 1 claims vs Day 3 claims ‚Üí Retention difference?
  • If Day 1 claimers have best retention ‚Üí Optimize for Day 1 display

Decision 3: Which content should we offer?

  • If action content has 60% completion but drama has 85% ‚Üí Offer drama content
  • Match content genre to user's preferences

Projected Impact (After Optimization in Month 2-3):

If special access works: Expand to more users ‚Üí Trial D7 retention +1-2pp
If special access doesn't work: Deprioritize ‚Üí Free up dev time for higher-ROI features
Either way: Data-driven decision instead of guess

Why Now? Special access is a major engagement bet. Without data, you're flying blind. Measure first, optimize second.
