üéØ Why This Initiative?

The Problem: 45% of users abandon videos before 90% completion. This is a retention killer because:

  • Users who complete videos have 2-3x higher Day-7 retention
  • Incomplete videos = unsatisfied users = churn
  • You're losing engagement from half your active users

Root Causes:

  • Slow start: 24 second median wait time ‚Üí users give up
  • Lost context: User starts video, exits app, forgets what they were watching
  • Friction: No easy way to continue where they left off
  • No momentum: Video ends ‚Üí user has to manually find next content ‚Üí drops off

The Solution: Reduce friction at every step of the viewing experience.

‚úÖ What We'll Do (Only Missing Features):

‚úÖ Already Implemented: Resume Watching (ContinueWatchingBloc + UI), Auto-Play Next (WatchNextNudge with 60s countdown), Skip Intro Button (skip_intro_button.dart). These are fully functional in production.

Gap Analysis: 3 of 5 features already exist. Only need buffering optimization and thumbnail progress indicators.

  • Resume Watching Banner: ‚úÖ ALREADY EXISTS - ContinueWatchingBloc fetches partially watched content, displays with progress bars
  • Auto-Play Next Content: ‚úÖ ALREADY EXISTS - WatchNextNudge shows countdown in last 60s, auto-redirects to next content
  • Skip Intro Button: ‚úÖ ALREADY EXISTS - skip_intro_button.dart detects intro segments, jumps to introEndTime
  • Optimize Video Buffering (NEW):
    • Current: 24s median startup time (TOO SLOW)
    • Target: <3s initial buffering
    • Actions: Optimize video CDN settings, implement adaptive bitrate selection, preload next chunk
    • Track: Add video_startup_time to playback events
    • Implementation: 5-7 days (requires CDN config + player optimization)
  • Progress Badges on Thumbnails (NEW):
    • Add visual "X% watched" badge overlay on all content cards
    • Show progress bar on thumbnail for partially watched content (10-90%)
    • Update: Home screen, search results, recommendation trays
    • Implementation: 3-4 days (UI updates across multiple screens)

Revised Effort: 8-11 days total (down from 2 weeks) because 60% of features already exist. Focus on buffering optimization and thumbnail UI enhancements only.

üìà How This Improves Funnels:

Video Completion Funnel Impact:

Video Start ‚Üí 90%: 55% ‚Üí Video Start ‚Üí 90%: 62%
Result: +7pp improvement = 14,314 more users completing videos (per month)

Downstream Funnel Effects:

  • D0 Consumption: Resume banner helps lapsed D0 users return ‚Üí 66% ‚Üí 68% (+2pp)
  • Trial D1 Retention: Users who complete 2+ videos have 35% higher D1 retention ‚Üí 27% ‚Üí 30% (+3pp)
  • Engagement Depth: Auto-play next increases videos per session ‚Üí Higher engagement score ‚Üí Better retention

Why Prioritize? Video completion is a leading indicator of retention. Fix this early ‚Üí compound effects throughout trial period.

üéØ Why This Initiative?

The Paradox: Special Access shows MIXED RESULTS that need investigation:

‚úÖ The Good: SA users activate trials at 9.15% vs 5.15% for non-SA (+4pp, +77.7% relative)
‚Üí This suggests SA is a powerful conversion tool

‚ö†Ô∏è The Concern: SA users cancel at 3.84% vs 2.17% for non-SA (+1.67pp, +77% relative)
‚Üí This suggests SA users might have lower intent or engagement quality

Critical Unknowns:

  • WHY do SA users cancel more? Are they just trying free content then leaving?
  • Which SA content converts best? Is it specific genres or shows that retain users?
  • WHEN to show SA? Does Day 0 vs Day 1 vs Day 3 timing affect retention?
  • What's the net effect? Does the +4pp activation outweigh the +1.67pp cancellation?
  • Drop-off points: Where do users abandon? Offer shown ‚Üí claimed ‚Üí watched ‚Üí completed

Business Impact:

  • If we fix cancellation: SA could be a major retention lever (scale to more users)
  • If cancellation is inherent: Optimize for high-intent users only (tighter targeting)
  • Without understanding WHY: We risk scaling a feature that churns users

The Solution: Deep-dive tracking to understand user behavior patterns and optimize accordingly.

üìà How This Improves Funnels:

Enables These Critical Decisions:

Decision 1: Should we scale SA or fix it first?

  • Scenario A: Net positive (119 activations - 50 cancellations = 69 net retained) ‚Üí Cautiously scale
  • Scenario B: High churn risk (if D7 retention is lower) ‚Üí Fix content/targeting first
  • Need to track: D7 retention for SA users vs non-SA users to get full picture

Decision 2: Which SA content types retain users best?

  • Track content ID, genre, and watch completion for SA claims
  • If specific genres (e.g., drama) have 85% completion vs action's 60% ‚Üí Prioritize drama
  • Identify "sticky" content that converts SA users to long-term subscribers

Decision 3: When to show SA in trial journey?

  • Current data: Day 0-7 mixed, need to segment by trial day
  • If Day 1 SA claims have better D7 retention than Day 0 ‚Üí Delay SA to Day 1
  • Track: Time from trial start to SA offer ‚Üí Optimal timing window

Decision 4: What drives the high cancellation rate?

  • Hypothesis 1: Users claim SA just for free content, no intent to pay ‚Üí Need better targeting
  • Hypothesis 2: SA content is low quality ‚Üí Improve content curation
  • Hypothesis 3: SA timing is wrong (too early/late) ‚Üí Optimize timing
  • Track claim-to-watch time, watch duration, and subsequent engagement to diagnose

Projected Impact (After Deep-Dive Analysis & Optimization):

Best Case: Fix cancellation issue ‚Üí SA activation stays 9.15%, cancellation drops to 2% ‚Üí Net +5pp trial retention
Mid Case: Better targeting ‚Üí SA activation 7%, cancellation 2.5% ‚Üí Net +3pp trial retention
Worst Case: Cancellation is inherent ‚Üí Tighten SA targeting to high-intent users only ‚Üí No net improvement but prevent bad user experience
Risk if we don't investigate: Scale a feature that brings in low-quality users ‚Üí Higher churn, worse unit economics

Why Now? The data shows a paradox: higher activation BUT higher cancellation. We must understand WHY before scaling SA to more users. This initiative prevents us from scaling a potentially leaky bucket.
